Hi. I'm Brett.

I'm a designer and engineer.

I've tried many hats...

As a young sprite, I bagged groceries and herded carts into their corrals.

Once I hit college, I spent a lot of time around the elderly.

For a brief stint, I was a custodian at my town's local library.

I was a tutor for some great kids.

Then I was a Peace Corps Volunteer in Namibia. For 26 months, I was a math teacher. I extended my service for a year. For the next 13 months, I developed teaching materials and collaborated with NIED staff and JICA volunteers to design the Information and Communication (IC) curriculum.

Next, I moved to Washington, D.C.

And became a Research Assistant at the United States Census Bureau.

Where I design and develop interactive data visualizations and write R and Python code to build simulations and conduct data analysis.

The Future

I love computer science. (Fingers crossed that graduate school is in my near future!)

And people (though I'm usually quiet or shy).

And data... Data and visual encodings are my clay.

I want to use technology to help people unlock their potential.

And to augment our abilities.

Especially compassion and empathy.

I think habits are key.
